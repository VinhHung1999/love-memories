# Brand & Voice: Love Memories

## 1. Brand Personality

### Brand Archetype: The Companion

Love Memories is not a coach (that is Paired), not an entertainer (that is Lovewick), and not a utility (that is Honeydue). Love Memories is a **companion** -- the trusted friend who helps a couple capture, organize, and celebrate their life together.

### Personality Traits

| Trait | Description | How It Shows Up |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| **Warm** | Genuine warmth without being saccharine | Soft color palette, encouraging microcopy, celebration of small moments |
| **Thoughtful** | Considers the details that matter | Scheduled letter delivery, location-tagged memories, relationship timer to the second |
| **Reliable** | Always there, works quietly in the background | Background uploads, push notification reminders, consistent daily experience |
| **Playful** | Lighthearted without being childish | Photo booth frames, "What to eat?" spinner, achievement badges, floating heart animations |
| **Private** | A vault for two, not a social network | No public profiles, no sharing features, no third-party data access |

### Brand Essence (One Sentence)

> "The private space where two people build a life together, one memory at a time."

### What Love Memories IS vs IS NOT

| Love Memories IS | Love Memories IS NOT |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| A companion | A therapist |
| A private space | A social network |
| Warm and encouraging | Clinical or academic |
| Beautifully simple | Feature-overwhelming |
| For capturing real life | For performing perfection |
| Vietnamese-born, globally minded | A Western app localized for Vietnam |

## 2. Tone of Voice

### Voice Principles

**1. Speak like a close friend, not a brand.**

```
YES: "You two had an amazing month! 12 moments captured."
NO:  "Your monthly activity report is ready for review."

YES: "Where should you eat tonight? Let's pick!"
NO:  "Utilize our AI-powered restaurant recommendation engine."
```

**2. Celebrate the everyday, not just milestones.**

```
YES: "That Tuesday pho run counts. Save it."
NO:  "Record your most memorable experiences."

YES: "100 days together -- that's a lot of pho bowls!"
NO:  "Congratulations on reaching your 100-day milestone."
```

**3. Be encouraging, never guilt-tripping.**

```
YES: "It's been a while! Ready to capture something new?"
NO:  "You haven't logged a moment in 14 days."

YES: "Your partner sent you something special."
NO:  "You have 1 unread letter. Please respond."
```

**4. Use Vietnamese cultural references naturally.**

```
YES: "Tet is coming -- time to plan something special together!"
YES: "Saved! Another food spot for the collection."
NO:  Generic "Happy holidays" messaging
```

### Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Onboarding** | Warm, guiding | "Welcome! Let's set up your shared space. First, invite your partner." |
| **Empty states** | Encouraging, aspirational | "No moments yet -- your story starts with the first one." |
| **Achievements** | Celebratory, playful | "Recipe Master unlocked! You've saved 10 recipes together." |
| **Notifications** | Gentle, personal | "A letter from [Partner Name] just arrived." |
| **Error states** | Honest, helpful | "Something went wrong saving your photo. We'll try again automatically." |
| **Monthly recap** | Reflective, warm | "What a month! Here's your story, told in moments." |
| **Upgrade prompts** | Value-focused, not pushy | "Want unlimited memories? Love Memories Plus keeps everything safe." |

## 3. Messaging Framework

### Level 1: Brand Narrative

**Why we exist**: Every couple creates thousands of small, beautiful moments -- but they get lost across camera rolls, chat threads, and forgotten bookmarks. Love Memories exists to give couples one private place where none of those moments disappear.

**What we believe**: The best relationships are built in the everyday -- in the Tuesday pho runs, the recipe experiments, the 11pm grocery lists, and the notes you leave each other. Those moments deserve a home.

**The transformation we enable**: From scattered memories and disconnected tools TO a unified, beautiful record of your life together.

### Level 2: Value Pillars

| Pillar | Benefit Statement | Proof Point | Customer Evidence (Target) |
|--------|------------------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **One Place for Everything** | Stop juggling 6 apps for your relationship | 14 modules: memories, food spots, recipes, goals, expenses, letters, dates, recaps | "I used to have photos in Google Photos, restaurants in Notes, and expenses in Splitwise. Now it's all here." |
| **Memories That Last** | Capture moments with photos, voice, music, and location -- not just a photo dump | Voice memos, Spotify links, location pins, Instagram Stories-style monthly recaps | "The monthly recap made us both tear up. We forgot how much we did together." |
| **Private by Design** | Your relationship data belongs to you two, period | No public profiles, no social features, no third-party data sharing, no ads | "I love that this is just for us. No one else sees our stuff." |
| **Made for Vietnamese Couples** | Built by someone who understands your culture, not translated from English | Vietnamese-first UI, local food culture, Tet integration, VN map support | "Finally an app that gets Vietnamese couples." |

### Level 3: Audience-Specific Messaging

| Audience | Primary Message | Secondary Message | CTA |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|-----|
| **Memory Keepers (22-30)** | "Never lose another moment together" | "Photos, voice memos, Spotify songs -- all in one timeline" | "Start saving memories" |
| **Organized Planners (25-35)** | "Your relationship, one app" | "Goals, expenses, food spots, date plans -- finally organized" | "Get organized together" |
| **Long-Distance (20-28)** | "Stay close, even far apart" | "Send letters, share memories, see your month together" | "Stay connected" |

## 4. Tagline Options

### Recommended (Primary)

> **"Your relationship, one app."**

**Why this works**: Clear value proposition. Immediately communicates the consolidation benefit. Positions against scattered tools, not competitors. Works in both English and Vietnamese.

**Vietnamese**: "Moi tinh cua ban, mot ung dung." (or adapt naturally: "Tat ca trong mot ung dung cho hai ban.")

### Alternatives (Test via A/B)

| Tagline | Positioning | Best For |
|---------|------------|----------|
| "One app for two" | Simplicity, exclusivity | App Store subtitle |
| "Where couples keep everything" | Utility, comprehensiveness | Landing page header |
| "Build your story together" | Emotional, aspirational | Social media campaigns |
| "Every moment. One place." | Memory-focused | Photo-centric marketing |
| "Made for two" | Private, intimate | Brand tagline alternative |

### Taglines to AVOID

| Tagline | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| "The best couples app" | Unsubstantiated; every competitor says this |
| "Strengthen your relationship" | Sounds like therapy (Paired's territory) |
| "Love made easy" | Trivializes relationships; feels like a dating app |
| "The couples super app" | "Super app" is overused and vague |

## 5. Emotional Positioning

### The Emotional Journey

```
BEFORE Love Memories:
- Memories scattered across 4 apps, can't find that restaurant from 3 months ago
- Partner asks "what should we eat?" and you both stare at each other
- Anniversary approaches and you realize you haven't planned anything
- "Remember that place we went?" — both open Google Maps and search separately
- Shared Google Sheet for expenses is ugly and nobody updates it

AFTER Love Memories:
- Open one app and see your entire relationship story
- "What to eat?" spinner picks a food spot from places you both loved
- Date planner has ideas ready; letters scheduled for delivery
- Food map shows every place you've eaten together, color-coded by rating
- Beautiful dashboard shows your relationship timer, recent moments, and active goals
```

### Emotional Triggers by Marketing Channel

| Channel | Emotional Trigger | Creative Direction |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Instagram/TikTok** | Nostalgia + FOMO | "POV: You open your monthly recap and see everything you did together" |

| **Valentine's Day** | Romance + gift-giving | "Give the gift of a shared space" / "Your love story deserves an app" |
| **Word-of-mouth** | Pride + sharing | "My girlfriend and I use this app and it's actually amazing" |

## 6. Brand Voice Guidelines for Content

### Do's

- Use contractions ("you'll", "it's", "we're") -- speak naturally
- Reference specific features as benefits ("save that food spot" not "utilize the location feature")
- Use "you" and "your partner" -- make it personal
- Include Vietnamese cultural touchpoints in seasonal content
- Celebrate small moments as much as big ones
- Use emoji sparingly and intentionally (hearts, food, celebration -- not walls of emoji)

### Don'ts

- Don't use corporate language ("leverage", "optimize", "utilize", "solution")
- Don't compare directly to competitors in user-facing content
- Don't guilt-trip about inactivity
- Don't over-promise ("fix your relationship", "never fight again")
- Don't use gendered language ("he/she" -- use "partner", "you two")
- Don't use "AI" as a marketing buzzword -- if AI features exist, describe the outcome, not the technology

### Writing Checklist

```
[ ] Would I say this to a friend? (If no, rewrite)
[ ] Am I celebrating something, not lecturing?
[ ] Is the value proposition in the first sentence?
[ ] Does this sound warm, not corporate?
[ ] Would this work in both English and Vietnamese?
```

## 7. Vietnamese Language Considerations

### Bilingual Strategy

- **App UI**: Vietnamese primary, English secondary (toggle in settings -- already implemented)
- **App Store listing**: Separate Vietnamese and English listings for each market
- **Social media**: Vietnamese for Vietnam market; English for diaspora and global
- **Landing page**: Vietnamese primary with English toggle

### Vietnamese Tone Considerations

Vietnamese communication tends toward:
- **Indirect warmth** over direct enthusiasm
- **Family-oriented framing** (relationship as building a future together)
- **Food as love language** (food features should be prominently marketed)
- **Modest celebration** over over-the-top hype

Adapt marketing tone accordingly: warm and encouraging in Vietnamese, slightly more energetic in English for international markets.
